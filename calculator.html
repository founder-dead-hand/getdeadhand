The Problem

The kitchen table is where jobs get lost.

You're standing there. Customer wants a number. Your truck's full of parts, your head's full of last week's invoices, and the price you quote in the next thirty seconds is the one you're going to honor for six hours of work tomorrow.

Quote too high, they call the next guy. Quote too low, you're eating the mistake all week. Write it on a scratch pad and half the time you forget what margin you used by the time you get to the invoice.

You've been doing this in your head for years, and you're good at it. But the customer wants to see the breakdown. They want a document, not a guess. And you want to be the guy who hands them something clean, not the guy fumbling in the truck for a clipboard.

How It Works

Four taps from walking in to quote delivered.

01

Enter Hours

How long is the job. That's it. Your rates are already set from the first time you opened the app. No dropdown. No "which labor type." No picker.

02

Add Materials

Tap, type the cost. Tap again, type the next one. Your margin's already applied. The total updates as you go. Reorder or remove with one swipe.

03

Present to the Customer

Flip the phone around. Presenter mode shows the number big, clean, defensible. Subtotal, tax, total. Done in front of them. No loading screen. No ads. Just the number.

04

Send the PDF

One button. Their email, your phone's share sheet, sent from your inbox. No login for them. No cloud service you pay for. No data trail you don't control.

What It Isn't

This isn't a CRM. It's a tape measure.

Dead Hand Calculator is a single-purpose tool. Quote fast, quote right, send clean. It will never become an invoicing engine, a scheduling app, or a customer database. If you need those, we make a different product.
