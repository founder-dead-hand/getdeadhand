THE PROBLEM

The phone is your biggest asset and your biggest headache.

You're busy because you're good at what you do. But being on a job means you're not on the phone. And when you're not on the phone, someone else is.

That water heater call you missed at 2pm on Friday? It went to whoever picked up. Not because they're better. Because they answered.

Answering services put someone between you and your customer. AI operators put a robot on your line. Neither of those is you — and your customers can tell.

HOW IT WORKS

A briefing in your hand before you've listened to a single voicemail.

01
CALL COMES IN

The system tries to bridge the call to you. If you're under a sink, it routes to your voicemail — recorded in your voice, in your words.

02
AI READS THE ROOM

The moment they hang up, AI triages the voicemail. Urgency tier. Job type. What they said, exactly. A reply draft ready to send.

03
YOU GET THE BRIEFING

Before you've heard the voicemail, you know who called, what they need, and how urgent it is. The draft is ready. You send it or edit it. Your words go out. Not a robot's.

04
LEAD IS YOURS

They hear back in minutes. The job is yours before they try anyone else. Close out the lead when it's done. The system moves on.

FROM THE FOUNDER

I built this because I was the guy missing the calls. Twenty years running a service operation, answering my own phone, losing jobs to whoever picked up first.

I tried answering services. They cost money and made me sound like a company I'm not. I tried AI operators. They were worse — callers could tell immediately, and I lost the job faster than if I'd just missed the call.

What I needed wasn't something to answer for me. I needed something that made sure I never forgot to answer. That ranked my queue so I knew which call to return first. That had a reply ready so I wasn't composing texts from a job site at 6pm.

This is that tool. It doesn't replace you. It makes sure the leads don't disappear while you're working.
